Poverty vs. Healthcare - Agust Erlingsson

Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

We found that the states with the lowest rates of poverty were New Hampshire (9.2), Maryland (10.1) , Connecticut at (10.8), and New Jersey (11.1). All of these states are in New England, all northern states, so geography may play a factor in poverty rates. The states with the highest poverty rates tended to be in the deop south, such as Kentucky (19.1), Lousiana (19.8), and Mississippi at an outstanding 21.5% poverty rate.

In terms of correlations, we found that lack of access to healthcare was primarily located in states with lower populations, like Idaho, Kansas, and Wyoming. However, Georgia and Florida, again two southern states, had alarmingly high levels of people without access to healthcare. And perhaps most shocking, especially for Texans, is that Texas tops the list of people without the ability to see a doctor, at a massive 24.9%. Nearly a quarter of the Lone Star state cannot get medical attention without paying a steep price. Texas was also among the highest in poverty, at 17.2.
